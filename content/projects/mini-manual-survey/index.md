We conducted a small survey to explore how students' sleep schedules related to how refreshed they felt. We collected the data with a Google Form and used the project as an example of manual data acquisition and exploratory visualization.

## Data Acquisition

We designed and distributed a Google Form that asked students about their sleep and how refreshed they felt. The responses gave us a compact dataset that we could clean, compare, and visualize.

## Results

We created two boxplots to view the relationship from both directions.

### Refreshed Feeling by Sleep Duration

The first plot compared students' refreshed ratings across sleep-duration groups.

## Takeaway

This small project gave us practice collecting data directly, structuring survey responses, and selecting visualizations that made group differences easier to inspect. Because the sample was small and self-reported, we treated the plots as exploratory rather than as evidence of a broader student population.
